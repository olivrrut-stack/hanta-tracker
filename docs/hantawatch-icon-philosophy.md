# Sentinel Dark

A philosophy of vigilance expressed through restraint. The visual language of continuous monitoring — deep dark fields punctuated by the precise, cold geometry of signal and warning. Form derived from the act of watching: grids, coordinates, the faint skeletal structure of a world under observation.

Space is not empty but charged. Dark grounds hold latent energy, ready to surface. The globe is not decoration but instrument — its meridians and parallels rendered as whisper-thin evidence of systematic mapping, the planet reduced to data and structure. Every line placed with the care of a cartographer who knows a life depends on the accuracy of the chart.

Color is surgical. Near-black carries the weight of the whole composition. A single red — precise, unsentimental, iOS system red — performs all the emotional work. This restraint demands mastery: the red must land perfectly, neither aggressive nor decorative, but clinical. The craft lies in knowing exactly how much signal is enough.

Scale and rhythm follow the logic of broadcast and reception. The alert marker expands outward in concentric rings — each one fainter, each one further — suggesting transmission, propagation, the invisible reach of a warning. This geometry of pulse is the meticulous work of someone who understands how information travels and what it means when it arrives.

The composition breathes. The globe occupies space without crowding it. Negative space is not waste but atmosphere — the dark around the sphere is the same dark as the app's map, making the icon feel like a window into the interface itself. Painstaking alignment and restraint, not decoration, is what gives this work its authority.
